Experimental Vocal Synthesis

Digital Signal Processing • Spectral Audio Analysis • Formant Modelling

Creating synthetic vocal instruments through spectral analysis, granular processing, formant modelling and custom audio workflows.

This final-year Music Technology dissertation explores how the human voice can be analysed, reconstructed and transformed using modern synthesis techniques. Across two original compositions, I developed a collection of experimental instruments and processing methods using Ableton Live, Max for Live, VoceVista Video Pro and custom DSP tools to investigate new approaches to vocal sound design.

About the Project

For my undergraduate dissertation, I investigated how the human voice could be reconstructed using synthesis rather than conventional vocal recording techniques.

Rather than treating vocals as recorded performances, the project analysed the voice as a collection of harmonic frequencies, formants and resonant spaces. Using spectral analysis, overtone extraction and multiple synthesis techniques, these individual components were rebuilt into entirely new instruments capable of producing expressive vocal timbres.

The project combined traditional synthesis with more experimental approaches, including Fourier-inspired harmonic reconstruction, granular synthesis, wavetable synthesis, EQ-based formant filtering, internal resonance recording and overtone resynthesis. VoceVista Video Pro was used to analyse the harmonic content of sung vowels, while Ableton Live acted as the primary environment for instrument design, modulation and composition.

Two contrasting compositions were produced throughout the project. The first explored these techniques within a structured musical arrangement, while the second focused on abstract sound design and the hidden harmonic architecture of the human voice.

Alongside the musical outcomes, the project also involved creating custom audio workflows, reusable Ableton devices and DSP processing chains that could be adapted for future vocal synthesis research and sound design applications.

Development Process

Research & Planning

Investigated vocal acoustics, source-filter theory, formant synthesis and spectral analysis to establish the technical foundation for the project.

Prototype Development

Designed and tested multiple synthesis workflows including wavetable instruments, granular processors, overtone mapping systems and EQ-based vocal modelling.

Composition & Iteration

Applied the developed techniques across two original compositions, refining instruments through continual listening, experimentation and supervisor feedback.

Final Production

Mastered both compositions, evaluated the effectiveness of each synthesis approach and documented the complete methodology within the accompanying dissertation.
