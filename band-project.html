About the Project

This project was designed to replicate the workflow of a professional recording session, taking an independent artist from initial planning through to a completed commercial release.

Working alongside a small production team, we recorded a four-track indie rock EP over multiple studio sessions, developing recording templates, organising session workflows, engineering tracking sessions and contributing to editing and mixing. The project also involved creating promotional material to accompany the release, including a behind-the-scenes documentary and online promotional content.

A major focus of my work centred on improving recording efficiency before entering the studio. Using the band's demo recordings, I produced complete Pro Tools session templates containing predefined tempos, arrangement markers, routing, FX returns and MIDI drum guides. This preparation allowed recording sessions to begin immediately, significantly reducing setup time and allowing more studio time to be spent capturing performances rather than configuring sessions.

Following recording, I contributed to the editing and mixing process, carrying out timing correction, gain staging, vocal tuning, corrective EQ, dynamics processing and effects routing to achieve a polished indie rock production. Throughout the project I developed a much stronger understanding of professional studio workflow, collaborative production and preparing recordings for commercial release.
